Activities

Virginia has countless activities that will surely be hit for families of all sizes and ages, such as hiking, horseback riding, golfing, camping, and so many more!

Hiking: Skyline Drive & The Shenandoah Mountains

Virginia has some of the most broad, and extensive, mountains on the East Coast. The highest point within the Shenandoah Mountains is approximately 4,050 feet. Skyline is a top-rated destination spot and is popular among many tourists that visit the location for hiking, lodging, and even getting a cup of coffee at the Skyland shops and food spots! A few of the hiking spots include Mary's Rock, Stony Man Mountains, Old Rag, The Appalachian Trail,and many more!

  1. Marys Rock, Elevation=3,514 feet, 3.4 miles in trail length
  2. Stony Man Mountain, Elevation=4,011 feet, 1.6 miles in trail length
  3. Old Rag, Elevation= 3,284 feet, 9 miles in trail length
  4. The Appalachian Trail, Elevation=6,643 feet, over 2,190 miles in trail length

Horseback Riding

Horses are a common sight in Virginia, and they are even included in many tourist destinations! Horseback riding is a popular hobby for even the most inexperienced! Destinations that offer this include the Skyline/Skyland area, the beaches a long the coast (Virginia Beach), and many private, and public, farms throughout the state. Most sites where this takes place offers all of the necessary tack for your riding experience to be great!

Golfing

Virginia has a variety of activities to take part in as we have come to know throughout this article, but an activity for the laid back ones, the older individuals, and ones who want to gain experience in a hobby/ sport can take part in the many wonderful golfing locations and resorts that Virginia has to offer! These top rated locations include resorts such as Kingsmill Resort (located in Williamsburg,Virginia), Drive Shack (located in Henrico, Virginia), The Hollows Golf Club (located in Montpelier, Virginia), and Heron Ridge Golf Club (Located in Virginia Beach, Virginia)
